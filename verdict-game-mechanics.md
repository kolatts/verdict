# 🔥 Verdict — Game Mechanics

## Concept
A hot take is put on trial. Players are randomly assigned to prosecute or defend it — regardless of their real opinion. The best argument wins. Your real opinion is exposed for drama.

---

## Room Setup

- Creator sets a **player name**, **number of rounds**, and **hot takes** (one per round)
- Hot takes can be selected from a **preloaded list**, typed as **custom takes**, or a mix of both
- Creator shares a **join link** — room and takes are **locked once the first player joins**
- Creator plays too

---

## Players

- No accounts — **GUID-based identity**, name entered on join
- Minimum players: **3**
- No maximum defined

---

## Round Flow

1. **Hot take revealed** — shown to all players simultaneously
2. **Side assigned** — each player is randomly assigned **Prosecution** or **Defense**; assignment is private until reveal
3. **Argument submitted** — each player writes a short argument (2–3 sentences) defending their assigned side
4. **Arguments revealed** — all arguments shown anonymously, labeled by side only (no names yet)
5. **Voting phase** — all players cast two independent votes:
   - ⚖️ **Best Argument** — who argued most convincingly (any side)
   - 👍 **Agree / Disagree** — their actual stance on the hot take
6. **Reveal** — arguments are de-anonymized; each player's real opinion is shown alongside what they argued
7. **Held in Contempt** — the player with zero Best Argument votes receives this badge for the round

---

## Scoring

- **1 point** per Best Argument vote received
- Agree / Disagree votes carry **no points** — drama only
- Scores are cumulative across all rounds
- **Leaderboard shown after the final round**

---

## Hot Takes — Preloaded List (starter set)

- Open offices were a war crime
- Lunch meetings should be illegal
- Remote work killed company culture
- Morning people are insufferable
- Networking events are purely performative
- Reply-all should be a fireable offense
- Standing desks are a personality, not a tool
- Group chats do more harm than good
- Unlimited PTO means zero PTO
- The office kitchen is a lawless zone

---

## End State

- Game ends after all rounds are complete
- Final leaderboard displayed with cumulative Best Argument points
- Held in Contempt badges shown per round
- No rematch mechanic defined — creator starts a new room to play again
